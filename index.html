Doyle Brunson

"The Godfather of Poker"

Here's some info on Doyle's exciting life:
  • In the 1950 Texas Interscholastic Track Meet, he won the one-mile event with a time of 4:43.
  • The Minneapolis Lakers of the NBA showed interest in Brunson, but a knee injury ended his hopes of becoming a professional basketball player.
  • Brunson has a master degree in Administrative Education
  • Brunson started his career by playing illegal poker games around Texas, Louisiana and Oklahoma. This is where he first meant Amarillo Slim and Sailor Roberts.
  • Besides his two championship wins in 1976 and 1977, Brunson's other Main Event cashes are: 1972 (3rd), 1980 (runner-up to three-time Main Event winner Stu Ungar), 1982 (4th), 1983 (3rd), 1997 (16th), 2004 (53rd), and 2013 (409th)
  • Brunson has 10 bracelets from WSOP events, tied for 3rd most.
  • As of 2023, his total live tournament winnings exceed $6,100,000.[3] He totaled over $3,000,000 in earnings from his 37 cashes at the WSOP.
  • Doyle wrote Super/System in 1978, one of the first books explaining poker strategy.
